## The Decline of the Left in the Twentieth Century: Towards a Theory of Historical Regression

*panelists include:* *[Chris Cutrone](http://platypus1917.home.comcast.net/~platypus1917/cutrone_20thcenturyleftdecline1917_061209.pdf),* *Atiya Khan,* *[Spencer Leonard](http://platypus1917.home.comcast.net/%7Eplatypus1917/leonardspencer_20thcenturyleftdecline2001_061209.pdf), & [Richard Rubin](http://platypus1917.home.comcast.net/%7Eplatypus1917/rubinrichard_20thcenturyleftdecline1940_061209.pdf); moderated by Benjamin Blumberg*

****â€¢**** The opening panel will elucidate significant moments in the progressive separation of theory and practice in the 20th and 21st Century history of Leftist politics: [2001](http://platypus1917.home.comcast.net/%7Eplatypus1917/leonardspencer_20thcenturyleftdecline2001_061209.pdf) (Spencer Leonard); 1968 (Atiya Khan); [1933](http://platypus1917.home.comcast.net/%7Eplatypus1917/rubinrichard_20thcenturyleftdecline1940_061209.pdf) (Richard Rubin); and [1917](http://platypus1917.home.comcast.net/~platypus1917/cutrone_20thcenturyleftdecline1917_061209.pdf) (Chris Cutrone). Each of these dates marked fundamental transformations on the Left. How do we relate to their legacies today? How has the problem of relating theory to practice, and ends to means, been dealt with politically on the Left? How has the political thought and action associated with each of these historical turning points revealed or obscured problems on the Left? How do the historical failures of the Left affect possibilities for the Left today and in the future?

The panel discussion will be followed with a Q & A session including the audience.

["The Left is Dead! -- Long Live the Left!" Vicissitudes of historical consciousness and possibilities for emancipatory social politics today](http://platypus1917.org/2007/11/01/vicissitudes-of-historical-consciousness-and-possibilities-for-emancipatory-social-politics-today/)

### Workshop

## An Unmet Challenge: Marxism and the Problem of Anti-Black Racism in the United States

*led by Benjamin Blumberg*

### **************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************room 707**************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************

****â€¢**** In this workshop, we will begin with Trotsky's warning made to the Socialist Workers Party (SWP) that the nonfulfillment to face the problem of racism in the United States will show the era's Marxist politics to have been a lie, or incoherent at best. We will examine how at the heart of the failure of American Marxism to bring about revolutionary change in the social, economic and ideological life of the American proletariat - and thereby to enormously advance the prospects of international revolution - was the inability to meet the challenge posed by the problem of racism.

## The End of Ideology Thesis: The American Left's Turn Towards the Right from the 1930s-50s

*led by Chris Mansour*

### **************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************room 707**************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************

## 

****â€¢**** In the mid 20th century, there was a tendency in the international intellectual scene--one which grew out of the Left--which asserted that the question of Marxism and class struggle began to exhaust itself, and in the near future Marxism would wither away as a political alternative. This tendency was exemplified by Daniel Bell's â€œThe End of Ideologyâ€ thesis: the idea that ideology in itself actually hindered progressive social change. In this workshop, we will investigate the historical roots of the end of ideology thesis, and attempt to understand the degeneration of American anti-Stalinist thinkers such as Daniel Bell and Irving Kristol, et al.

### Roundtable

## The Left Around the World: Canada, India/Pakistan, Germany, and Mexico

### **************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************room 707**************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************************

*panelists: Ryan Hardy (Canada), Atiya Khan (India/Pakistan), Jerzy Sobotta (Germany), & Marco Torres (Mexico); moderated by Spencer Leonard*

****â€¢**** In the Communist Manifesto, Marx famously wrote, â€œThe working men have no country.â€ At the same time, he noted that the form the universal struggle for freedom would take in the age of proletarian capitalist was precisely international - â€œThough not in substance, yet in form, the struggle of the proletariat with the bourgeoisie is at first a national struggle. The proletariat of each country must. . ., first of all settle matters with its own bourgeoisie.â€ This roundtable discussion brings together panelists with significant political experience outside the United States to discuss the question of the tension between national and international historical conceptions of the Left. Each panelist will give an account their sense of the characteristic forms the Death of the Left is taking in the country of which they speak, identifying any groups or tendencies who register any awareness of the prevailing regression on the global Left today. Of particular interest will be the question of (decline of) internationalism. The ambition of the panel is to at least begin re-imagining what the reconstitution of Left Internationalism might look like in the 21st century.
